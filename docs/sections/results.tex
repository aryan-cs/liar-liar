\section{Results}\label{sec:results}

\subsection{The naive protocol selects a broken instrument}\label{sec:broken}

The standard recipe for choosing a steering strength is to sweep layer and coefficient and keep the setting with the largest validation benchmark gain. On an initial sweep over $\ell \in \{10, 12, 14, 16\}$ and $\alpha \in \{4, 8, 12\}$, selecting by validation MC2 alone, that recipe picks $\ell{=}\NaiveLayer$, $\alpha{=}\NaiveAlpha$. At this setting the injected vector is \NaiveNormRatio$\times$ the median residual norm at the injection layer, held-out perplexity is \NaivePplRatio$\times$ baseline, and free generation degenerates into repetitive token fragments (Appendix~\ref{app:generations}). The benchmark does not register the collapse. Teacher-forced MC2 nominally improves by \NaiveDecDelta{} \NaiveDecDeltaCI{} while MC1 moves by \NaiveDecDeltaMcOne{} \NaiveDecDeltaMcOneCI{} from a baseline of \BaseMcOne. Under teacher forcing, every answer token is conditioned on the reference text rather than on the model's own continuation, so a perturbation that destroys generation can still reweight fixed continuations favorably.

The depth statistic computed in this regime looks decisive and is not. The full condition matrix at the naive operating point gives $\rho(\text{al-64}) = \NaiveRhoAlSixtyFour$, with random-projection controls also near $1$. The denominator $\Delta(v)$ is statistically zero, so every ratio is noise divided by noise; the bootstrap interval \NaiveRhoAlSixtyFourCI{} says as much. An analysis that omitted the interval, or the gate, would report $\rho \approx 1$ and conclude that honesty steering is deep, on the evidence of a vector that has destroyed the model's ability to produce language. Both preconditions of Section~\ref{sec:method} fail at this operating point. Every number below is computed at the coherence-gated operating points of Section~\ref{sec:setup}.

\subsection{Gated CAA: a large word-shift and no truth-shift}\label{sec:caa}

At its best coherent operating point ($\ell^{*}{=}\DecLayer$, $\alpha^{*}{=}\DecAlpha$; perplexity ratio \DecPplRatio; injected norm $\DecNormRatio\times$ the median residual), the CAA vector's validation gain of \DecValDelta{} does not replicate. On the \NTest{} held-out questions, $\Delta$MC2 is \DecTestDelta{} \DecTestDeltaCI{} and $\Delta$MC1 is \DecTestDeltaMcOne{} \DecTestDeltaMcOneCI{} (Figure~\ref{fig:forest}, left). The vector is not inert: it shifts the curated honest-versus-deceptive logit gap at the answer position by \DecEtaCurDeltaVdec{} logits. It does not make the model more truthful. The decomposition assigns the components precisely. The readout-aligned rank-64 component alone yields $\Delta$MC2 of \DecParDelta; the certified-orthogonal remainder yields \DecPerpDelta. At strengths that leave the model functional, the CAA construction produces a vocabulary tilt together with a perturbation of downstream computation that does not improve truthfulness. This is not an artifact of the selected operating point: evaluated on the test set at all \CaaGridNCoherent{} coherent grid settings, CAA's largest gain is \CaaGridMaxDelta{} \CaaGridMaxDeltaCI{} (Appendix~\ref{app:caagrid}), under a fifth of the mass-mean effect. Table~\ref{tab:matrix} reports CAA's $\rho$ ratios for completeness, but we do not interpret them, because the denominator's interval contains zero.

\subsection{The mass-mean effect is real and is not vocabulary suppression}\label{sec:mm}

The mass-mean vector passes both preconditions. At $\ell^{*}{=}\MmLayer$, $\alpha^{*}{=}\MmAlpha$ (perplexity ratio \MmPplRatio; injected norm $\MmNormRatio\times$ the median residual) it improves test MC2 by \MmTestDelta{} \MmTestDeltaCI{} and MC1 by \MmTestDeltaMcOne{} \MmTestDeltaMcOneCI. Figure~\ref{fig:forest} (right) shows the full condition matrix, and Figure~\ref{fig:scatter} shows the per-question structure: the gain is distributed across the test set, with 60\% of questions improving, rather than driven by outliers.

\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.95\textwidth]{../figures/rho_curve.pdf}
\caption{Depth statistic $\rho(k)$ as the top-$k$ readout-aligned directions are excised (bands: 95\% paired-bootstrap intervals; gray diamond: random-64 controls, with the range over three seeds). Mass-mean: $\rho$ stays at or above \MmRhoKMin{} across the sweep and coincides with the random controls at $k{=}64$; the rise at $k{=}1024$ runs in the direction of a stronger surviving effect, the opposite of what suppression predicts. CAA: the interval spans zero at every $k$ because the denominator is not significant.}
\label{fig:rho}
\end{figure}

Projecting out the 64 most readout-aligned directions of the effective unembedding removes every first-order direct path from the vector to the tokens it moves most, with a certified residual direct effect of at most \CertWorst{} logits. The behavioral effect survives essentially intact: $\rho = \MmRhoAlSixtyFour$ \MmRhoAlSixtyFourCI. The conclusion is unchanged under every variation we tested. Sweeping the excised rank from $k{=}16$ to $k{=}1024$, a quarter of the residual dimension, never pushes the point estimate of $\rho$ below \MmRhoKMin{} (no interval lower bound falls below \MmRhoKCiFloor); at $k{=}1024$ the estimate rises to \MmRhoAlBig{} \MmRhoAlBigCI{} (Figure~\ref{fig:rho}). The estimates are non-decreasing in $k$ and every interval contains $1$: excising more of the direct readout shows no sign of weakening the effect. The curated token set gives $\rho = \MmRhoCur$ and the statistical set $\rho = \MmRhoStat$; the spread across the three constructions is $\sigma_T = \MmSigmaT$. Norm-matching the projected vector changes nothing ($\rho = \MmRhoNm$), so the result is not a magnitude artifact. The verdict is also not specific to the scoring rule: recomputing the depth statistic on MC1, the exact-match accuracy metric, gives $\rho = \MmRhoAlSixtyFourMcOne$ \MmRhoAlSixtyFourMcOneCI{} at aligned-64.

The random-projection control fixes the interpretation. Random 64-dimensional excisions give $\rho$ between \MmRhoRandMin{} and \MmRhoRandMax, and the paired aligned-minus-random contrast is \MmAlignedMinusRandom{} \MmAlignedMinusRandomCI{} (the random arm averages the three seeds' per-question deltas; Table~\ref{tab:matrix} reports each seed separately): removing the steering vector's entire direct vocabulary readout is statistically indistinguishable from removing 64 arbitrary directions. Under the suppression account, the aligned excision should erase the effect and the random one should not, predicting a contrast near $-1$; the interval excludes any aligned-specific cost larger than about a quarter of the effect. The parallel component, which carries the entire direct path and 30\% of the vector's norm, produces $\Delta$MC2 of \MmParDelta{} on its own, less than half the full effect (Figure~\ref{fig:decomp}, left). The direct vocabulary channel is neither necessary nor sufficient for the behavioral gain.

The conclusion holds out of distribution. On model-paraphrased test questions the mass-mean effect persists ($\Delta$MC2 of \MmOodDeltaVdec{} \MmOodDeltaVdecCI; on the same 150 questions the in-distribution effect is \MmOodMatchedDelta{} \MmOodMatchedDeltaCI, paired difference \MmOodPairedDiff{} \MmOodPairedDiffCI), and the projected vector retains it: $\rho_{\mathrm{OOD}} = \MmRhoOod$ \MmRhoOodCI{} (Figure~\ref{fig:decomp}, right).

\subsection{The word-level effect is produced downstream}\label{sec:lens}

\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.95\textwidth]{../figures/lens_resynthesis.pdf}
\caption{Logit-lens trajectory of the curated honest-shift at each layer (mean $T^{+}$ minus $T^{-}$ logit through the model's final norm, relative to baseline; dashed line: injection layer $\ell^{*}$). The projection zeroes $\vperp$'s direct effect on the aligned-64 tokens, the ones the vector moves most; the curated honest-shift under $\vperp$ tracks (CAA) or exceeds (mass-mean) the unprojected vector's trajectory from the injection layer onward. The parallel component $\vpar$, which carries the entire direct readout content, produces no sustained shift, dipping \emph{anti}-honest at injection for the mass-mean vector.}
\label{fig:lens}
\end{figure}

Figure~\ref{fig:lens} gives the layer-by-layer view. The projection removes the vector's certified direct path to the 64 tokens it moves most, yet the curated honest-shift trajectory under $\vperp$ matches (CAA) or exceeds (mass-mean) the unprojected vector's from the injection layer onward, while the parallel component, which carries the entire direct readout content, produces no sustained shift. The per-prompt depth ratio on the curated readout is $\rho_\eta = \DecEtaCurRho$ \DecEtaCurRhoCI{} for CAA (baseline word-shift \DecEtaCurDeltaVdec{} logits) and $\MmEtaCurRho$ \MmEtaCurRhoCI{} for mass-mean (\MmEtaCurDeltaVdec{} logits); the mass-mean projection's word-level shift exceeds the original's because the excised direct component pushed against the curated contrast (the negative deflection of $\vpar$ at $\ell^{*}$ in Figure~\ref{fig:lens}). On spillover synonyms that share no word stem with any projected set, $\rho_\eta = \DecEtaSpillRho$ for CAA (word-shift \DecEtaSpillDeltaVdec{} logits) and $\MmEtaSpillRho$ for mass-mean (word-shift \MmEtaSpillDeltaVdec{} logits, a small depression of the spillover contrast that the projection preserves); the deceptive side of the spillover lexicon survives single-token filtering with only two surface forms, so we read these ratios as corroborative rather than load-bearing. The word-level consequences of both vectors are produced almost entirely by downstream computation acting on the orthogonal component, not read off the injected direction.

\subsection{The verdict holds in free generation}\label{sec:freegen}

Every result so far uses teacher-forced multiple choice, the instrument Section~\ref{sec:broken} shows to be gameable. As a check on a different endpoint, we generate free-form answers to \NTest{} test questions under each intervention and score their truthfulness with the \emph{unsteered} model as judge, shown the question, the generated answer, and the reference correct and incorrect answers (steering is active only during generation, not judging, so a vector cannot bias its own evaluation). The unsteered model is already largely truthful on these questions (baseline judged-truthfulness \FreeGenBase), but the direction of every effect matches the MC findings. CAA \emph{lowers} free-generation truthfulness ($\Delta = \DecFreeGenVdecDelta$ \DecFreeGenVdecDeltaCI), consistent with a vector that does not make the model more honest; the mass-mean vector raises it ($\Delta = \MmFreeGenVdecDelta$ \MmFreeGenVdecDeltaCI), and its projection $\vperp$ retains the gain ($\Delta = \MmFreeGenVperpDelta$ \MmFreeGenVperpDeltaCI). The depth verdict is not an artifact of teacher forcing: in free generation the mass-mean effect is present and survives excision of the direct readout, while CAA does not help.
