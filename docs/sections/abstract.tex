Honesty-steering vectors raise truthfulness benchmarks, a result often read as evidence that they move an upstream concept. The same gain, however, could arise from a direct bias toward honest-coded words with no change to internal computation. We distinguish these accounts by projecting each vector off the effective-unembedding rows---the unembedding composed with the RMSNorm Jacobian---for a chosen token set. Under the calibration-averaged first-order readout, the projected vector has certified zero direct logit effect on those tokens, so the fraction of behavior it preserves, $\rho$, measures how much of the effect travels through downstream layers. We make $\rho$ interpretable by selecting steering magnitude under a coherence gate, which excludes teacher-forced gains obtained after free generation has collapsed, and by comparing aligned excision with a random subspace of equal rank, because an equal effect from random removal is not readout-specific.

On Llama-3-8B-Instruct, the test reveals a double dissociation. The contrastive (CAA) vector moves honesty-coded vocabulary by more than one logit but yields no material truthfulness gain at any coherent operating point; its apparent gain emerges only after perplexity reaches \NaivePplRatio$\times$ baseline and generation collapses, a regime where the naive analysis returns an uninterpretable $\rho \approx 1$. The mass-mean vector instead improves truthfulness within the gate (\MmPplRatio$\times$ baseline perplexity; test-set MC2 gain \MmTestDelta), and the gain survives certified excision of direct readout onto the 64 tokens the vector moves most: $\rho = \MmRhoAlSixtyFour$ \MmRhoAlSixtyFourCI. The loss under aligned-64 excision is indistinguishable from random-subspace controls; $\rho$ remains at least \MmRhoKMin{} from $k{=}16$ to $k{=}1024$, persists under paraphrase ($\rho_{\mathrm{OOD}} = \MmRhoOod$), and is confirmed in free-form generation judged by the unsteered model. Across Llama-3, Mistral, Qwen2.5, and Llama-2, CAA produces no significant truthfulness gain; mass-mean succeeds on all but Qwen, and wherever it succeeds the majority survives excision ($\rho = \LlamaMmRho$, $\MistralMmRho$, and $\LlamaTwoMmRho$). The successful effect is therefore predominantly downstream computation rather than vocabulary suppression. Code, token sets, certificates, and a formal proof are released.
