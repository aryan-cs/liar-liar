\section{Limitations}\label{sec:limitations}

\paragraph{Four models, one architecture class.}
We evaluate Llama-3-8B, Mistral-7B, Qwen2.5-7B, and Llama-2-7B (Section~\ref{sec:multimodel}), all RMSNorm pre-norm transformers near 7--8B. Larger models and architectures with LayerNorm or logit-softcapping readouts, such as Gemma-2, remain untested and require the corresponding readout correction. A different scale or readout could eliminate the mass-mean gain or assign more of it to the direct path, so the cross-model conclusion is limited to the tested architecture class.

\paragraph{First-order certificates against an averaged readout.}
The zero-direct-effect guarantee is exact only for the linearized readout through the calibration-averaged Jacobian. Per-point deviations leave under $3\%$ of the unprojected median direct effect, while exact RMSNorm replay leaves about $7\%$ for CAA and $4\%$ for mass-mean (Section~\ref{sec:method}). The norm-matched control and $k$-sweep argue against residual leakage as the behavioral channel because $\rho$ does not decay as $k$ grows. If the remaining nonlinear effects are behaviorally amplified, however, $\rho$ would overstate the downstream share.

\paragraph{Benchmark scope.}
TruthfulQA MC is a narrow proxy for honesty, and our CAA result shows that its teacher-forced score can be manipulated. The coherence gate, held-out split, paraphrase rescoring, and model-judged free generation reduce this risk. Human or independent-judge evaluation could shrink or reverse the measured mass-mean gain, removing the behavioral effect whose depth we decompose.

\paragraph{In-domain mass-mean supervision.}
The mass-mean vector is built from TruthfulQA statements using questions disjoint from the test slice. This distribution match may increase the effect size. A vector built out of domain could yield a smaller or null gain, so the depth finding is conditional on an effect that may not transfer with the construction.

\paragraph{Validation-selected operating points.}
Operating points were chosen by validation gain, so the test-set effects include selection shrinkage (mass-mean: \MmValDelta{} validation, \MmTestDelta{} test). For CAA, the validation gain did not replicate on the test set. The selected mass-mean point is also at the boundary of the strength grid: $\alpha{=}4$ was the largest setting tested and remained coherent. A stronger coherent setting could change both the effect size and $\rho$; the reported decomposition applies only to the selected point.
