\section{Generalization across models}\label{sec:multimodel}

We repeat the protocol on Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.3 \citep{jiang2023mistral}, Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct \citep{qwen2025qwen25}, and Llama-2-7B-chat \citep{touvron2023llama2}. The RMSNorm-corrected construction is unchanged, but each model has its own coherence-gated operating point. Table~\ref{tab:multimodel} and Figure~\ref{fig:multimodel} report the results alongside Llama-3.

\paragraph{At selected operating points, CAA yields no statistically significant positive gain.}
At each coherence-gated operating point, CAA's test-set $\Delta$MC2 is \LlamaDecDelta{} \LlamaDecDeltaCI{} on Llama-3, \MistralDecDelta{} \MistralDecDeltaCI{} on Mistral, \QwenDecDelta{} \QwenDecDeltaCI{} on Qwen, and \LlamaTwoDecDelta{} \LlamaTwoDecDeltaCI{} on Llama-2. The Mistral effect is significantly negative; the other three are indistinguishable from zero. CAA therefore fails at the behavioral stage at these selected points, despite its vocabulary-level signal on Llama-3. Because there is no statistically significant positive gain to decompose, its $\rho$ values cannot distinguish suppression from downstream computation and are not interpreted.

\paragraph{The mass-mean effect replicates on three of four models.}
Mass-mean improves truthfulness on Llama-3 (\LlamaMmDelta{} \LlamaMmDeltaCI), Mistral (\MistralMmDelta{} \MistralMmDeltaCI), and Llama-2 (\LlamaTwoMmDelta{} \LlamaTwoMmDeltaCI). On Qwen, the effect is indistinguishable from zero (\QwenMmDelta{} \QwenMmDeltaCI). Qwen provides no statistically detectable gain, so uniform transfer across the tested models is not established. Panel (b) of Figure~\ref{fig:multimodel} therefore reports $\rho$ only for the three models where the statistically significant positive effect replicates.

\paragraph{Where the effect exists, it is predominantly downstream relative to aligned-64.}
Most of each successful mass-mean gain survives certified aligned-64 readout excision: $\rho = \LlamaMmRho$ \LlamaMmRhoCI{} on Llama-3, $\rho = \MistralMmRho$ \MistralMmRhoCI{} on Mistral, and $\rho = \LlamaTwoMmRho$ \LlamaTwoMmRhoCI{} on Llama-2. None approaches $\rho \approx 0$, as pure vocabulary suppression predicts. This is the shared decomposition result: on each responsive model, most of the gain survives removal of the tested calibration-averaged first-order aligned-64 contribution. The aligned-minus-random comparison then measures whether the removed readout subspace matters more than generic equal-rank damage. On Llama-3, it does not (aligned$-$random $= \LlamaMmAmr$ \LlamaMmAmrCI, not significant). Mistral shows a larger aligned-specific cost (aligned$-$random $= \MistralMmAmr$ \MistralMmAmrCI), removing roughly one quarter of the effect. Llama-2 is intermediate, with a smaller, marginal cost (aligned$-$random $= \LlamaTwoMmAmr$ \LlamaTwoMmAmrCI). The gain is therefore predominantly downstream relative to aligned-64 on all three responsive models, but the aligned readout contribution is model-dependent and is not claimed to be zero.
