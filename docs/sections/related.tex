\section{Related work}\label{sec:related}

Our method draws on activation steering, concept erasure, and unembedding geometry. Prior work establishes the behavioral phenomenon, the form of the intervention, and the relevant readout path, but does not combine them into a behavioral decomposition.

\paragraph{Activation steering.}
Representation engineering adds contrastively constructed vectors to the residual stream to modulate high-level behavior \citep{zou2023repe}. Contrastive activation addition \citep{rimsky2024caa} and inference-time intervention \citep{li2023iti} apply this approach to honesty-related behavior. \citet{arditi2024refusal} show that refusal is mediated by a single direction that can be removed from every writing matrix. Steering vectors often generalize poorly out of distribution \citep{tan2024analysing}. Our depth statistic tests whether their effects pass through the direct readout or downstream computation.

\paragraph{Concept erasure.}
Our intervention draws on concept erasure. LEACE \citep{belrose2023leace} gives the minimum-norm affine map that removes linear decodability of a concept, following iterative nullspace projection \citep{ravfogel2020inlp} and rank-constrained adversarial erasure \citep{ravfogel2022rlace}. Our projection has the same structure, but reads the erased subspace from the model's effective unembedding rather than learning it from labels. It constrains the direct logit contribution on a token set to zero rather than constraining probe accuracy.

\paragraph{Unembedding geometry.}
Our use of unembedding rows builds on the context/unembedding geometry formalized by \citet{park2024linear}. \citet{marks2024geometry} construct truth directions from mass-mean differences, which we use as the alternative steering vector. Direct logit attribution follows \citet{elhage2021mathematical} and \citet{nostalgebraist2020logitlens}.

\paragraph{Closest precedents.}
Three works overlap most directly. \citet{venkatesh2026nonid} prove that steering vectors are non-identifiable up to perturbations in the null space of the full activation-to-logit Jacobian. Our subspace restricts their construction to the readout and measures the behavioral consequences of this ambiguity. \citet{nadaf2026function} show that function vectors can steer behavior that the logit lens cannot decode, establishing an off-readout channel for in-context learning. The benchmark of \citet{hughvd2024benchmark} implements $\WU$-orthogonal steering for sentiment on Gemma-2-9B. None of these studies applies the construction to honesty steering, corrects for the RMSNorm Jacobian, or reports a certified quantitative depth decomposition.
