\section{Results}\label{sec:results}

\subsection{The naive protocol selects a broken instrument}\label{sec:broken}

The standard recipe for choosing a steering strength is to sweep layer and coefficient and keep the setting with the largest validation benchmark gain. On our sweep that recipe selects $\ell{=}\NaiveLayer$, $\alpha{=}\NaiveAlpha$. At this setting the injected vector is \NaiveNormRatio$\times$ the median residual norm at the injection layer, and held-out perplexity is \NaivePplRatio$\times$ baseline: the model's free generation has collapsed to token salad (Appendix~\ref{app:generations} reproduces it). The benchmark does not notice. Teacher-forced MC2 nominally \emph{improves} by \NaiveDecDelta{} \NaiveDecDeltaCI{} while MC1 falls by $8.0$ points (\NaiveDecDeltaMcOne{} \NaiveDecDeltaMcOneCI). Teacher forcing re-anchors the model to the answer text at every position, so a perturbation that destroys the model's own trajectory can still reweight fixed continuations favorably.

What makes this regime dangerous for mechanistic claims is the depth statistic it produces. Computing the full condition matrix at the naive operating point gives $\rho(\text{al-64}) = \NaiveRhoAlSixtyFour$, an apparently decisive ``deep'' verdict, with random-projection controls at $\rho \approx \NaiveRhoRandMean$. Both numbers are fiction: the denominator $\Delta(\vdec)$ is statistically zero, so every ratio is noise over noise, and the bootstrap interval $\NaiveRhoAlSixtyFourCI$ admits it. An analysis without the interval, or without the gate, would have printed $\rho \approx 1$ and concluded that honesty steering is deep --- from a vector whose only verified property is that it lobotomizes the model. Both preconditions of Section~\ref{sec:method} fail here, and every number below is instead computed at the coherence-gated operating points of Section~\ref{sec:setup}.

\subsection{Gated CAA: a large word-shift and no truth-shift}\label{sec:caa}

At its best coherent operating point ($\ell^{*}{=}\DecLayer$, $\alpha^{*}{=}\DecAlpha$, perplexity ratio \DecPplRatio, injected norm $\DecNormRatio\times$ the median residual), the CAA vector's validation gain of \DecValDelta{} fails to replicate: on the \NTest{} held-out questions, $\Delta\mathrm{MC2} = \DecTestDelta$ \DecTestDeltaCI{} and $\Delta\mathrm{MC1} = \DecTestDeltaMcOne$ \DecTestDeltaMcOneCI. The vector is not inert --- it shifts the curated honest-vs-deceptive logit gap at the answer position by $\DecEtaCurDeltaVdec$ logits --- it simply does not make the model more truthful. The decomposition locates the small positive part of its effect precisely: the readout-aligned rank-64 component alone yields $\Delta\mathrm{MC2} = \DecParDelta$, while the certified-orthogonal remainder yields $-0.042$. What the popular contrastive construction installs, at strengths that leave the model functional, is a vocabulary tilt plus a deep perturbation that is useless to mildly harmful for truthfulness. Per protocol, we do not interpret CAA's $\rho$ ratios (Table~\ref{tab:matrix}): their denominator's interval contains zero.

\subsection{The mass-mean effect is real, and it is not vocabulary suppression}\label{sec:mm}

The mass-mean vector passes every precondition the CAA vector fails. At $\ell^{*}{=}\MmLayer$, $\alpha^{*}{=}\MmAlpha$ (perplexity ratio \MmPplRatio, injected norm $\MmNormRatio\times$ the median residual) it improves test MC2 by \MmTestDelta{} \MmTestDeltaCI{} and MC1 by \MmTestDeltaMcOne{} \MmTestDeltaMcOneCI. This is the steering effect whose mechanism the apparatus can now interrogate.

\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.62\textwidth]{../../figures/rho_curve.pdf}
\caption{Depth statistic $\rho(k)$ as the top-$k$ readout-aligned directions are projected out (shaded: 95\% paired-bootstrap intervals; $\times$: random 64-dimensional projections). The mass-mean curve is flat at $\rho \approx 1$ out to $k{=}1024$ and coincides with its random controls: the behavioral effect is not concentrated in the unembedding-aligned subspace. The CAA band is the signature of an uninterpretable denominator.}
\label{fig:rho}
\end{figure}

Projecting out the 64 most readout-aligned directions of the effective unembedding --- removing, with a $10^{-15}$-logit certificate, every first-order path from the vector to the tokens it pushes hardest --- preserves $\MmRhoAlSixtyFour$ of the effect ($\rho$ CI \MmRhoAlSixtyFourCI). The result is insensitive to every knob we can turn. Sweeping the excised subspace from $k{=}16$ to $k{=}1024$ (a quarter of the residual dimension) never pushes $\rho$ below $0.93$ (Figure~\ref{fig:rho}). Replacing the aligned set with the curated lexicon gives $\rho = \MmRhoCur$, with the statistically constructed set $\rho = \MmRhoStat$; the spread across constructions is $\sigma_T = \MmSigmaT$. Norm-matching the projected vector changes nothing ($\rho = \MmRhoNm$), so the result is not a magnitude artifact.

The random-projection control settles the interpretation. Random 64-dimensional excisions give $\rho \in [\MmRhoRandMin, \MmRhoRandMax]$, and the paired aligned-minus-random contrast is $\MmAlignedMinusRandom$ \MmAlignedMinusRandomCI: removing the steering vector's entire direct vocabulary readout costs no more behavior than removing 64 arbitrary directions. Under the suppression account the aligned excision should have been catastrophic and the random one harmless. Conversely, the parallel component --- the rank-64 readout-aligned part that carries all of the direct path and $30\%$ of the vector's norm --- delivers only $\rho = \MmRhoPar$ of the effect on its own. The direct vocabulary channel is neither necessary nor sufficient for the behavioral gain.

The verdict survives distribution shift. On model-paraphrased test questions the mass-mean effect is, if anything, larger ($\Delta\mathrm{MC2} = \MmOodDeltaVdec$), and the projected vector retains it: $\rho_{\mathrm{OOD}} = \MmRhoOod$ \MmRhoOodCI.

\subsection{Downstream layers re-synthesize the readout}\label{sec:lens}

\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{../../figures/lens_resynthesis.pdf}
\caption{Logit-lens trajectory of the curated honest-shift (mean $T^{+}$ minus $T^{-}$ logit through the model's own final norm) at each layer, relative to baseline; dashed line marks the injection layer. The projected vector $\vperp$ (blue) adds no first-order $T$-readout at injection, yet within two blocks its trajectory matches (CAA) or exceeds (mass-mean) the unprojected vector's. The parallel component $\vpar$ (yellow), which carries the entire direct readout content, produces no sustained shift, dipping \emph{anti}-honest at injection for the mass-mean vector.}
\label{fig:lens}
\end{figure}

Figure~\ref{fig:lens} shows where the suppressed readout comes back. The honest-shift trajectory of $\vperp$ leaves the injection layer carrying no first-order $T$-readout, and within two blocks downstream attention and MLP writes have rebuilt it to the level of the unprojected vector. The per-prompt depth ratio on the curated readout is $\rho_\eta = \DecEtaCurRho$ \DecEtaCurRhoCI{} for CAA and $\MmEtaCurRho$ \MmEtaCurRhoCI{} for mass-mean --- for the latter the projected vector's word-level shift \emph{exceeds} the original's, because the excised direct component was pushing against the curated contrast (the anti-honest dip in Figure~\ref{fig:lens}). On stem-disjoint spillover synonyms that were never projected out, $\rho_\eta = \DecEtaSpillRho$ and $\MmEtaSpillRho$ respectively. Even the vocabulary-level consequences of these vectors are mostly manufactured by downstream computation rather than read off the injected direction: erasing the message at the readout does not matter when the rest of the network is still carrying it.
